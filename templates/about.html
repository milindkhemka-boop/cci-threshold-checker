How the figures are built

Methodology & sources

The exchange rate

For converting foreign-currency assets and turnover, the CCI uses the average of the RBI reference rates over the relevant trailing period of six months. This tool scrapes the daily reference rates (USD, GBP, EUR, JPY, AED, IDR) from the RBI Reference Rate Archive, stores them locally, and computes the simple average over the trailing {{ meta.fx_window_days }} days. JPY (quoted per 100) and IDR (per 10,000) are normalised to “INR per 1 unit”.

The numbering systems

Indian filings report in lakh / crore; international documents in million / billion. The tool reads and displays both, on the equivalences 1 crore = 10 million, 100 crore = 1 billion, 1 lakh crore = 1 trillion. Every amount is shown in both systems, e.g. ₹2,500 crore (= 25.00 billion).

The thresholds (Section 5, Competition Act 2002)

This build reflects the MCA threshold revision of 7 March 2024 and the Deal Value Threshold / CCI (Combinations) Regulations 2024 in force from 10 September 2024:

  • Jurisdictional — parties: India assets > ₹2,500 cr or turnover > ₹7,500 cr; or worldwide assets > USD 1.25 bn / turnover > USD 3.75 bn (each with an India leg of ₹1,250 cr / ₹3,750 cr).
  • Jurisdictional — group: India assets > ₹10,000 cr or turnover > ₹30,000 cr; or worldwide assets > USD 5 bn / turnover > USD 15 bn (same India legs).
  • Small-target / de minimis exemption: target assets in India ≤ ₹450 cr or turnover ≤ ₹1,250 cr — unavailable where the deal value exceeds ₹2,000 cr.
  • Deal Value Threshold: deal value > ₹2,000 cr and the target has Substantial Business Operations in India.
  • SBOI: India turnover or GMV ≥ 10% of global and > ₹500 cr; or India digital users ≥ 10% of global.

Turnover is taken as revenue from operations (excluding other income and indirect taxes); values are book values from the audited accounts of the financial year preceding the transaction.

How a filing decision is indicated

A combination is flagged as likely notifiable if either a jurisdictional limb is crossed (and the small-target exemption does not rescue it) or the deal-value threshold is met with SBOI. This mirrors the structure of the Act but is a screening indicator only.
